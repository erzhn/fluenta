Create: app/(dashboard)/ielts-speaking/page.tsx

This is a full IELTS Speaking mock exam with 3 parts. Use:
- `speak()` and `stopSpeaking()` from '@/lib/speech' — examiner reads questions aloud
- `createRecognition()` from '@/lib/speech' — records user answers via microphone
- Groq API via '/api/ai' or a new '/api/ai/ielts-feedback' route for AI scoring

UI: dark glass style, max-w-2xl mx-auto, mobile-friendly

──── PART 1: Introduction (4–5 min) ────
Show topic + question. Examiner reads it aloud automatically via speak().
User presses mic button → createRecognition() records their answer → auto-stops after silence (8 seconds of no speech via onEnd).
After each answer show a brief skeleton/spinner then display simple tips.
Questions bank (use these hardcoded, randomly pick 3–4 per session):

Topic: "Home"
- Describe your home. What is it like?
- Do you live in a house or a flat?
- What do you like most about your home?

Topic: "Work/Study"
- Do you work or are you a student?
- What do you enjoy most about your job or studies?
- Do you think your work or studies will change in the future?

Topic: "Free Time"
- What do you like to do in your free time?
- Has your idea of free time changed since you were younger?
- Do you prefer spending your free time alone or with others?

Topic: "Travel"
- Do you like travelling?
- What is your favourite place you have visited?
- Would you like to live in another country?

──── PART 2: Long Turn (3–4 min) ────
Show a "cue card" with a topic + 4 bullet points.
User gets 60 seconds to prepare (countdown timer displayed).
Then speaks for up to 2 minutes (countdown timer).
After speaking, show AI feedback button.

Cue cards bank (pick 1 randomly per session):
1. "Describe a skill you would like to learn"
   • What it is
   • Why you want to learn it
   • How you would learn it
   • How it would benefit you

2. "Describe a memorable journey or trip"
   • Where you went
   • Who you went with
   • What you did there
   • Why it was memorable

3. "Describe a person who has influenced you"
   • Who this person is
   • How you know them
   • In what way they influenced you
   • Why they are important to you

4. "Describe a book, film or TV show you enjoyed"
   • What it is called
   • What it is about
   • Why you chose it
   • What you learned or felt

──── PART 3: Discussion (4–5 min) ────
2–3 follow-up discussion questions related to the Part 2 cue card topic.
Each question auto-spoken by TTS. User answers via mic (max 90 seconds each).

Sample Part 3 questions (linked to the 4 cue cards above):
For skill: "Do you think it is ever too late to learn a new skill?" / "How has technology changed the way people learn?" / "Which skills do you think will be most valuable in the future?"
For journey: "Why do people enjoy travelling?" / "Do you think tourism has a negative impact on local cultures?" / "How might travel change in the next 20 years?"
For person: "What qualities make a good role model?" / "Do celebrities have a responsibility to be role models?" / "Who do you think influences young people the most today?"
For book/film: "Do you think reading is becoming less popular?" / "How has streaming changed the entertainment industry?" / "Should art and literature be taught in schools? Why?"

──── FEEDBACK ────
After all 3 parts, user can press "Получить оценку" button.
Call Groq API with prompt: evaluate IELTS Speaking with scores in 4 criteria (Fluency & Coherence, Lexical Resource, Grammatical Range & Accuracy, Pronunciation) each 1-9, plus overall band score (average), and 3 concrete tips for improvement.
The user's transcript for each answer is accumulated in state and sent to AI.

Display result card with:
- Overall band score (large, colored: <5 red, 5-6.5 amber, 7+ green)
- 4 criteria scores as progress bars (0-9 scale)
- 3 improvement tips as bullet list
- Button "Попробовать снова"
